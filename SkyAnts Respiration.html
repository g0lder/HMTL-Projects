Cellular Respiration

Cellular respiration is the chemical process of turning food molecules into ATP, or adenosine triphosphate. ATP is the energy used to power all cell processes, and is even used to make more ATP and break down C6H1206, or glucose, through a process called glycolysis.



Cellular respiration takes place inside of the mitochondrion, cellular respiration uses glucose and oxygen, and produces carbon dioxide, water, and glucose. (6CO2 and 6H2O)


Anaerobic Respiration

Anaerobic respiration is when a cell performs cellular respiration in the absence of oxygen (O and O2). When this happens, the mitochondrion uses glycolysis to break down the glucose, and creates byproducts like water, carbon dioxide,and energy (ATP).



Typically, this process does not generate as much energy as its counterform, aerobic respiration, but it can happen at almost any time.



Aerobic Respiration

Aerobic respiration is the process of turning food products into ATP. The two materials used in aerobic respiration are glucose and oxygen. If no energy is wasted, than this method can produce a staggering 34 ATP, whereas most anaerobic respiration produces only 2 ATP.



Aerobic respiration is common in most organisms, as all plants, fungi, and animals use it; however, there are not any known bacterium or archaea have any membrane bound organs like the mitochondrium which is required for this type of respiration.




Glycolysis

Glycolysis is when glucose molecules are broken down in the cytoplasm of the cell. This can only occur in the presence of oxygen,as this process relies on oxidization.





Glycolysis results in 4 different end products, 4 ATP, 2 pyruvate, and 2 NADH; however, 2 ATP is used, and so is 2 NAD+, so the net gain is only 2 ATP and some NADH. Pyruvate is not very important, but is often used to synthesize more glucose, wheras NADH is crucial in metabolizing energy, and ATP is the energy storage used.


Examples

Anaerobic Respirators

  • Bacteriums
  • Archea
  • Certain types of fungi
  • Humans can temporarily produce oxygen anaerobically
  • All prokaryotes (single-celled organisms)



Aerobic Respirators

  • All animals
  • All plants
  • Most fungi
  • All other eukaryotes (multi-cellular organisms)




Extra Facts About Cellular Respiration


  • Anaerobic respiration occurs in your intestines, and one of the byproducts is actually flatulence. (Excess gas escaping your body ;)
  • Some cells, like yeast, create alcohol as a byproduct.
  • It is likely that the entire process of cellular respiration takes only a few milliseconds, by comparison, blinking usually takes between one to four hundred milliseconds!
